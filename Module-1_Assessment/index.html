About Me

I am seeking a post-college career that utilizes my highly developed analytical skills, an ability to focus deeply on tasks, and my strong teamwork orientation. I will be a Bachelor of Sciences graduate from the College of Biological Sciences at the University of Minnesota in May 2020 with an additional minor in Spanish. My skills have been developed and demonstrated at school, in the workplace, and in my hobbies.

Some fundamental experiences that have shaped the person I am include studying at the Fundación Ortega – Marañón in Toledo, Spain and taking field classes on Mammology and Animal Behavior at Itasca State Park, Minnesota. I am interested in culture, travel, and the ecological interactions between animals and their environments.

I am also skilled in the Japanese skill toy kendama, and have been playing since 2013. I was awarded second place out of 100 contestants at the North American Kendama open in the AM division in November 2019.
